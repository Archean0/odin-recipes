Oatmeal Raisin Cookies

Description

Oatmeal raisin cookies are a personal favorite of mine and quick to make.

If you are looking for something a little different, try using cranberries and walnuts in place of the raisins.

All of the ingredients are minimally processed and easy to find in most grocery stores.

Ingredients:

  • 3/4 cup butter
  • 3/4 cup white sugar
  • 3/4 cup light brown sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 1/4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 3/4 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 2 3/4 cups rolled oats
  • 1 cup raisins

Steps:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375 degrees Fahrenheit.
  2. Line two cookie sheets with parchment paper or silicone liners.
  3. In a large bowl, beat butter, white sugar, and brown sugar until smooth and creamy.
  4. Beat in the eggs and vanilla extract until fluffy.
  5. In a medium bowl, combine flour, baking soda, cinnamon, and salt.
  6. Gradually beat the dry mixure (the medium bowl) into the butter mixure (the large bowl).
  7. Stir in the oats and raisins.
  8. Drops teaspoonfuls of batter 2 inches apart on the prepared cookie sheets.
  9. Place the cookie sheets on the center rack of the oven until golden brown--about 8 to 10 minutes.
  10. Remove the cookie sheets from the oven and allow them to cool for 1 to 2 minutes.
  11. Remove the cookies from the cookie sheets and place them onto wire racks to cool completely.
  12. Serve and enjoy! (Try it with a glass of cold milk or hot spiced tea!)
